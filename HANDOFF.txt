NEW STRUCTURAL-GENERALIZATION SWEEPS (IN PROGRESS, HIT A SNAG)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Per the "generalize beyond one oil-exporter calibration" recommendation,
built two comparative statics (both scale Omega^F_i import-cost shares,
with ALPHA_i absorbing the change so Cobb-Douglas shares still sum to 1):

1. Import-intensity level (kappa in {0.25,...,2.5}): scales the whole
   import-share vector proportionally.
2. Import-exposure heterogeneity (theta in {0,...,1.5}): holds total
   import intensity fixed, varies how UNEVENLY it's spread across
   sectors -- the direct quantitative test of the Gamma proportional to B
   theorem.
